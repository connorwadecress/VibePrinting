# Editorial Plan

## Recommendation

Do not start with "completely random facts."

That makes the pipeline harder to score, harder to improve, and harder for YouTube to understand. Start with one channel thesis and three repeatable lanes inside it.

## Recommended channel thesis

`Compressed Curiosity`

Thirty-to-forty-five second Shorts that explain surprising history, human performance, and everyday science through a fast narrative payoff.

## Initial content lanes

### 1. History flash

Format:

- hook with an unexpected fact
- one historical setup beat
- one reversal or contrast
- one memorable closing line

Examples:

- why Roman concrete lasted so long
- the real reason marathon distance is weird
- how ancient armies moved faster than people think

### 2. Human limits

Format:

- question about speed, strength, endurance, perception, or survival
- answer with one clean benchmark
- explain what it means in practice

Examples:

- how fast elite sprinters actually move
- what a marathon does to the body
- how long you can hold your breath compared with trained divers

### 3. Everyday systems

Format:

- "what actually happens when..."
- mechanism in plain language
- counterintuitive reveal

Examples:

- what turbulence really is
- why microwaves heat unevenly
- how GPS knows where you are

## Why this is the right starting shape

- broad enough to generate volume
- narrow enough to train a recognizable channel identity
- low dependence on current news
- compatible with stock visuals and generated scenes

## Content template

Each Short should aim for:

- `1` hook
- `1` thesis sentence
- `3-5` supporting beats
- `1` payoff
- `1` CTA, usually curiosity-driven rather than sales-driven

## Cadence

Recommended daily batch:

- `08:00` generate and hold candidate set
- `11:00` publish slot 1
- `15:00` publish slot 2
- `19:00` publish slot 3

Keep the third slot optional until quality is stable.

## Backlog rules

- keep at least `30` scored topic candidates ready
- do not publish near-duplicate hooks within 14 days
- rotate lanes so the channel does not feel monotone
- retire weak formats instead of endlessly tweaking them

## Title and thumbnail posture

For Shorts, title quality still matters, but the first second matters more.

Bias toward:

- concrete stakes
- specific numbers
- unusual contrast
- one surprise, not three

Avoid:

- generic "did you know"
- fake urgency
- claims the script cannot support

## Quality bar

A video should be held if any of these fail:

- hook is weak by second two
- narration sounds synthetic in a distracting way
- visuals do not track the story beat
- claim confidence is unclear
- the piece feels like filler instead of a payoff
